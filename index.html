What do we cover in our workshops?

  • Software Development
  • Hardware and Robotics

Our software workshops cover all the essential tools that you will need in order to create functional and effective software (no experience required).

Examples of what we have covered in pervious workshops include:

Web Development

Web Development is an extremely important skill to know when participating in hackathons. Good web development requires you to combine your knowledge of software and design in order to produced a finished product that not only works as intended but looks visually appealing as well.

Software Principles and Testing

Our Software Principles and Testing workshops cover what it takes to write functional, effective software. We teach the basics on how to format your code, improve readability, and test your code under various circumstances.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

A hackathon is a design sprint-like event, usually hosted by a tech company or organization, in which computer programmers and others involved in software development, including graphic designers, interface designers, media developers, and others get together for a short period of time to collaborate on a software project. The events generally only last 24 to 36 hours and take place over a weekend.

Absolutely not! Hackathons are open to students of any skill level and often feature beginner-friendly workshops led by industry leaders. There are mentors readily available who are open to any questions you may have and will gladly assist you in whatever it is that you want to learn.

At the end of the hackathon, every team's projects are judged. There are of course the standard first, second, and third place winners but most hackathons also select winners under different criteria such as "Best first-time hack" or "Best use of ___ in their project". Common prizes include Google Nests, Occulus Rifts, Arduinos, Drones, Headphones, etc. However, your motivation for participating in a hackathon should not be for the prizes. Rather, you should be motivated to learn something new and most importantly have fun!

We participate in monthly hackathons as part of the club however, you are encouraged to participate in hackathons during your own time. Normally you would be restricted to hackathons physically taking place in Ontario but because of the COVID-19 pandemic, most hackathons are open to everyone virtually. Major League Hacking (MLH) holds multiple hackathons every week which you can find at https://mlh.io/seasons/2021/events

For hackathons done as a part of the club, we will assign teams with a balanced skill level. For hackathons that you do in your own time, you can either come with a pre-organized team or you can find a team during the event. Most hackathons will have a place where you can create teams if you do not already have one.

Every Thursday from 3-4 PM there will be a workshop. These workshops will teach you a skill that will benefit you when you participate in a hackathon. After each workshop there is an optional project that you can complete to test your knowledge. Each month we participate in a MLH hackathon (virtually) where you can apply what you have learned. You are also encouraged to do hackathons on you own time, MLH hosts multiple hackathons each week which you can find here.
